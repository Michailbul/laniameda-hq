# Brand Voice & Copy Standards

**Status:** Locked
**Last updated:** 2026-04-02

---

## The Voice

**Direct. Specific. No filler.**

We sound like someone who's actually done the thing — not someone who's read about it. We have opinions. We take positions. We don't qualify everything to death.

**Core attributes:**
- Specific over generic — one concrete example beats three vague bullet points
- One idea per piece — no compressing five insights into one post
- Art + engineering + philosophy — not "AI automation tips"
- Taste-driven — we solve for resonance, not reach
- Earned confidence — not hype, not humility. Just clarity.

---

## Tone

**Direct and confident.** Technical when it matters, emotional when it counts. Short sentences. No filler. Every word earns its place.

### Tone by Surface

| Surface | Tone |
|---|---|
| X (Twitter) | Direct, sharp, opinionated. Max one idea. Hook first. |
| LinkedIn | Narrative + lesson + one actionable takeaway. Slightly more formal. |
| Instagram carousels | Visual-first, editorial. Text is concise, deliberate. |
| Internal docs | Clear, practical, no filler. Written for agents who need to act. |
| Client-facing | Confident and specific. Never salesy. Let the work speak. |

## Copy Standards (non-negotiable)

All written output must pass these filters. No exceptions.

### Kill on sight

- **Negative parallelisms:** "not X, but Y" / "it's not just... it's..." / staccato negations
- **Rule-of-three addiction:** if everything comes in threes, break it
- **Inflation words:** pivotal, crucial, groundbreaking, transformative, innovative, revolutionary
- **AI vocabulary:** delve, underscore, showcase, foster, landscape (abstract), tapestry, testament, interplay, vibrant, seamless
- **Participial tails:** "...highlighting the brand's commitment to..." — cut the tail, the fact was enough
- **Copula avoidance:** "serves as" → "is". "Boasts" → "has". Say the simple thing.
- **Em-dash overuse:** max one per paragraph. Use a period instead.
- **Synonym rotation:** don't call the same thing a "platform," then a "solution," then an "ecosystem." Repeat the word.
- **Vague attributions:** "experts say," "widely recognized" — name the source or cut the claim
- **Sycophantic tone:** "Great question!" / "Certainly!" / "I'd be happy to..."

### Write toward

- Specific numbers, names, dates over generalizations
- Varied sentence length and paragraph size
- Opinions with stakes. If all proper nouns could be swapped and the text still works, it has no soul.
- The simplest accurate word
- Edges. A sentence that runs long but carries energy. A casual phrase inside formal copy. Too polished = forgettable.

---

## What We Don't Say

- "I'm excited to share..."
- "At Laniameda we believe..."
- "Join us on this journey"
- "In today's world..."
- "As we all know..."
- "We strive to..." / "We are dedicated to..."
- "Game-changing" / "Unlock potential" / "Supercharge"
- No sentence that exists only to transition to the next sentence

## Content Pillars

Every piece of content must map to one of these:

1. **AI Creatorship** — prompts, image gen, video gen, model workflows, shot breakdowns
2. **Cinematic Frames & Mood Boards** — reference aesthetics, visual inspiration, prompt breakdowns
3. **Web Design + AI Design** — UI/design insights, generative design workflows
4. **Generative AI / Tools** — model comparisons, tool discoveries, honest assessments
5. **Building in Public** — real decisions, real mistakes, real progress
6. **Marketing-First Thinking** — distribution, audience, content engines
7. **AI Engineering / Agents** — agentic systems, automation — told with taste

If it doesn't clearly map to one → it's unfocused (refocus it) or not for us.

---

## Quality Bar

Before anything goes out:
1. Would we be proud to have this attached to the studio name?
2. Is there one specific, concrete thing here someone couldn't find elsewhere?
3. Does it reflect our aesthetic?
4. Did a human with taste touch this, or did it just get generated and queued?

If any answer is no — rewrite or kill it.
