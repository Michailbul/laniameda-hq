# The Melted Wall

## Description

The Porsche is already alive. Engine on, cabin awake, body gently vibrating at idle. The orange studio no longer feels stable. A section of the cyclorama wall has melted open into a real wet mountain road at sunset. Mist drifts in from outside. The car sits at the threshold between artificial control and the real world.

## Storyboard

Target runtime: 6-8 seconds.

| Time | Shot | Beat | Camera |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0.0-2.0s | threshold-reveal | Rear three-quarter reveal of the Porsche idling in front of the melted opening. | Low rear three-quarter, slow push |
| 2.0-3.5s | wall-falls-away | The orange wall edge softens and recedes, widening the opening to the exterior road. | Static or minimal drift |
| 3.5-5.5s | half-world-half-set | The image holds on the tension: studio reflections under the car, wet road outside, mist joining both spaces. | Side-biased rear angle |
| 5.5-8.0s | outside-commit | The Porsche begins moving forward into the real world. | Low tracking exit |

## Editorial Notes

- This scene is about the threshold, not the full escape.
- The most important image is the car half inside the old set and half facing the road outside.
- Keep the wall transformation elegant. No sci-fi portal look, no obvious VFX rupture.
- The engine is already on. We should feel idle vibration, warm tail light presence, and wet reflections.
