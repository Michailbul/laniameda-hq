# Shot Brief 06 — Wildlife Intercuts

> **Purpose:** The soul beats. Quick cuts to the forest reacting to the intrusion. Not comedy — empathy. The forest was here first. These moments breathe between the speed.

> **Duration in edit:** 1-3 seconds each. Used as punctuation between car shots, not as standalone sequences.

> **Seedance stress test:** Animal motion, nature physics (birds, leaves, water), very short required action — can Seedance nail a micro-moment?

---

## The Philosophy

These are NOT cute animal shots. They're REACTION shots. Every wildlife intercut is a response to the cars — vibration, noise, air displacement. The animals are secondary characters experiencing the race from their world.

In the edit, these drop in as 1-2 second flash cuts between car shots. They control pacing — a bird scattering between two tracking shots creates a breath, a rhythm change.

---

## Intercut Menu — Pick 2-3 for the Film

### INT-A — "Birds Scatter"
**Frame:** Medium shot of a tree branch with 3-4 small birds perched. Forest behind, road below out of focus.
**Action:** The birds react simultaneously — heads snap toward the road, then they burst off the branch in different directions. Leaves shake from their departure. Branch sways.
**Duration needed:** 1.5 seconds.
**Why:** Universal visual shorthand for "loud thing happened." Instant read.
**Difficulty:** Medium. Seedance should handle birds leaving a branch — it's a fast, explosive motion with clear physics.

### INT-B — "Squirrel Bolt" (mid-film callback)
**Frame:** Squirrel on a tree trunk, mid-frame, gripping bark. Forest floor below. Similar to the branch-lookout squirrel image you already have.
**Action:** Squirrel freezes flat against the bark (fear posture) → then bolts straight up the trunk and out of frame. Bark particles scatter from its claws.
**Duration needed:** 2 seconds.
**Why:** Callbacks to the opening squirrel. The audience recognizes it (or a squirrel like it). Connection.
**Difficulty:** Medium-high. Vertical movement up a trunk is specific.

### INT-C — "Puddle Shockwave"
**Frame:** Close-up of a still puddle on the road shoulder or forest floor. Reflected trees visible in the water surface.
**Action:** The puddle vibrates — concentric ripples spread from engine vibration transmitted through the ground. The reflection shatters into fragments. A single leaf drops into the disturbed water.
**Duration needed:** 2 seconds.
**Why:** No animal needed. Pure physics poetry. The vibration of the approaching cars reaches the tiniest scale. Also: tests water surface physics in Seedance.
**Difficulty:** Medium. Water ripple is a well-understood physics simulation.

### INT-D — "Deer Freeze" (if you want a bigger animal moment)
**Frame:** Through the trees — a deer standing in dappled light, 20 meters from the road. Soft focus foreground branches.
**Action:** The deer's head snaps up from grazing. Ears rotate. It stares toward the road for a frozen beat. Doesn't run — just watches. Alert stillness.
**Duration needed:** 2 seconds.
**Why:** Gravity. A deer is a bigger emotional anchor than a squirrel. The frozen stare is powerful — it's dignified, not panicked.
**Risk:** Deer are harder to generate convincingly than squirrels. The anatomy needs to be right or it pulls you out.
**Alternative:** Skip the deer entirely. The squirrel carries the wildlife story alone.

### INT-E — "Leaves Vortex"
**Frame:** Road-level, looking across the asphalt. Leaves and debris on the road shoulder.
**Action:** A blast of air (car wake) rips the leaves off the ground. They spiral upward in a vortex, hang in the air for a beat, then scatter and settle. No car visible — just the aftermath of one passing.
**Duration needed:** 2 seconds.
**Why:** The car doesn't need to be in frame for you to feel its presence. The leaves ARE the car's signature. Ghost of speed.
**Difficulty:** Leaf physics. This is a pure Seedance physics test — vortex dynamics, settling, air behavior.

---

## How to Use in Edit

These are INTERCUT beats, not a scene. They go between car shots:

```
[Car tracking shot] → [1.5s birds scatter] → [Car overtake shot] → [2s puddle ripple] → [Car tunnel shot] → etc.
```

They work like percussion hits. The car shots are the melody. The wildlife cuts are the drums.

**Rule:** Never put two wildlife cuts back-to-back. Always sandwich them between car shots.

## Recommendation

**Must-have:** INT-C (puddle shockwave) — easiest to generate, most visually unique, pure physics test.

**Should-have:** INT-A (birds scatter) or INT-E (leaves vortex) — one "disruption" beat that shows force without showing the car.

**Nice-to-have:** INT-B (squirrel bolt) — if the opening squirrel works, this mid-film callback lands.

**Skip unless ambitious:** INT-D (deer) — beautiful idea but anatomically risky. Save for a future longer cut.

---

## Notes

- These are the EASIEST shots to generate in isolation. Small frame, simple action, short duration. Good for quick wins between the harder car shots.
- Generate these as a batch session — knock out 4-5 start frames in Nano Banana, then run them all through Seedance in one sitting.
- Sound design does 50% of the work on these. The visual just needs to be clean. Audio sells the cause-and-effect.
