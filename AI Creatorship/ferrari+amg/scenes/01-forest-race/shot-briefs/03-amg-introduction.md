# Shot Brief 03 — AMG Introduction (The Mirror Reveal)

> **Purpose:** Introduce the AMG GT3 not as an equal — but as a threat arriving. The audience meets it through the Ferrari's perspective. The mirror is a window into what's coming. This turns a two-car chase into a story: the hunter appears.

> **Duration in edit:** 4-5 seconds

> **Seedance stress test:** Reflection rendering with a moving secondary subject inside the reflection. Detail-level close-up. Vibration/micro-shake.

---

## The Core Idea

We're on the Ferrari. We've been watching it alone. Then we cut to its side mirror — and in the reflection, headlights are growing. The AMG is closing. The reveal is earned through tension, not exposition.

---

## Variations

### VAR A — "Side Mirror, Headlights Growing" (the pitch)
**Start frame:** New — extreme close-up of Ferrari's left side mirror. Mirror reflects the road behind: forest, haze, and faint headlights in the distance. The mirror housing is red metallic, slightly motion-blurred at edges. Road streaks in the reflection.
**Action:** Headlights in the mirror grow brighter and larger — the AMG is gaining. Mirror vibrates from speed (subtle shake). Reflection sharpens as the AMG gets closer — we start to make out the dark shape, the wing silhouette. The headlights split into the AMG's distinctive dual beams.
**Camera:** Locked on mirror. No camera movement. The entire story happens inside the reflection.
**Why this works:** The audience leans in — the mirror is small, the information is compressed. It's how a real driver would first notice a car catching them. Cinematic restraint.
**Risk:** Seedance rendering a coherent, evolving reflection is genuinely hard. The mirror is small in frame, detail inside it needs to track. This is a coin-flip shot.
**Fallback:** If reflection doesn't resolve, use it as a 2-second flash cut — even a partially-working mirror shot reads if it's fast enough.

### VAR B — "Cockpit Over-Shoulder to Mirror"
**Start frame:** New — cockpit interior, over the driver's left shoulder. Steering wheel partial in foreground, left side mirror visible through the window. Forest blurs past the window. Dashboard ambient glow.
**Action:** Camera is inside the car. Through the window, in the side mirror, headlights appear. The driver's gloved hand adjusts on the wheel (tension tell). Mirror vibrates.
**Camera:** Static interior, over-shoulder POV.
**Why this works:** Adds human presence without showing a face. The hand on the wheel is universal — anyone who drives knows that moment. Also: you already have the Ferrari cockpit/gauges image, so interior rendering has precedent.
**Risk:** Multiple layers of complexity: interior + window + mirror + reflection + hand movement. A lot for one Seedance clip. May get confused.
**Simplification:** Drop the hand movement. Just cockpit POV → mirror → headlights grow.

### VAR C — "Rearview Mirror, Center"
**Start frame:** New — interior, center rearview mirror fills most of the frame. Through the mirror: the road behind, forest corridor, haze. Empty.
**Action:** Hold on empty mirror for 1-2 seconds. Then: two points of light emerge from the haze in the reflection. They grow. The shape of the AMG materializes — dark, wide, wing prominent. The reflection gets sharper as it closes distance.
**Camera:** Static, locked on rearview mirror.
**Why this works:** Rearview is larger than side mirror = more reflection area for Seedance to work with. Higher chance of legible detail. Also more cinematic — it's the classic "check your mirror" thriller beat.
**Risk:** Interior needs to be dark/minimal so the reflection reads. If interior is too detailed, model splits attention.

### VAR D — "The Shortcut" (if mirrors fail)
**Start frame:** Existing Ferrari rear tracking shot (solo).
**Action:** Camera is tracking Ferrari from behind. In the background, deep in the haze — a dark shape emerges. AMG headlights pierce the mist. It's gaining. By end of clip the AMG is visible but still distant. A silhouette with intent.
**Camera:** Rear tracking on Ferrari, AMG enters the deep background.
**Why this works:** No reflection rendering needed. The reveal is spatial — the AMG literally materializes from the fog. Still earns the "oh, there's a second car" moment. Just differently.
**Risk:** Lowest risk of all options. The model just needs to render a car approaching in background while tracking another. Well within Seedance capability.
**Tradeoff:** Less clever than the mirror concept, but guaranteed to work.

## Start Frame Strategy

| Variation | Start frame | Complexity | Need to generate? |
|-----------|------------|------------|-------------------|
| A | Ferrari side mirror close-up with faint headlights in reflection | High | Yes — Nano Banana, very specific |
| B | Cockpit over-shoulder, mirror through window | Very high | Yes — may need multiple attempts |
| C | Interior, center rearview mirror | Medium-high | Yes |
| D | Ferrari rear tracking, empty road behind | Low | Have it or minor regen |

---

## Recommendation

**Try VAR A or C first** — they're the vision. Give it 2-3 attempts. If the reflection doesn't hold, **VAR D is the safe landing** and still works narratively.

VAR D is not a compromise — it's a different storytelling choice. "Emerging from the mist" is as cinematic as "appearing in the mirror." Just less technically ambitious.

The smart play: generate VAR D as your guaranteed fallback FIRST (30 min), then spend creative time on VAR A/C knowing you already have a working version.

---

## Notes

- AMG identity in the reflection doesn't need to be perfect — we just need: dark shape + wing silhouette + dual headlights. The audience fills in the rest.
- If using mirror shots: the mirror housing should be red metallic (Ferrari body color). Don't over-describe it — just "red metallic mirror housing."
- The AMG is NOT fully visible in this shot. It's a tease. Full reveal comes in the next shot (chase together).
- Sound design in post will do heavy lifting here — engine note growing, closing distance. The visual just needs to suggest "something's coming."
