# Core Forest Chase

## Thesis

Ground the film in photoreal automotive chase language first. The camera should feel mounted, handheld, or roadside in ways a real car commercial crew would use. The forest supplies atmosphere and stakes. The cars supply speed and contrast.

## Visual Grammar

- Rear chase tracking as the dominant language.
- Static roadside pass-bys to make speed feel physical.
- Detail inserts for texture: wheel, exhaust, mirror, puddle, leaves.
- Low western backlight, haze, rim light, lifted teal shadows.
- Damp asphalt for Scene 01, with reflections and tire spray.

## Use When

- Establishing the cars.
- Selling speed.
- Testing Seedance continuity and car identity stability.
- Building footage that can survive in a final edit without needing explanation.

## Avoid

- Video-game chase energy.
- Camera moves that feel impossible without a motivated rig.
- Too many actions in one clip.
- Over-describing what the start frame already shows.
