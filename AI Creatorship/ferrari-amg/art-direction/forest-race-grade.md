## Light Direction

Sun is low from the west, behind or behind-above the cars in most chase angles. Rim light reads on rooflines, wing edges, mirrors, and tire spray. Diagonal god rays cut through canopy haze. Mist and airborne debris glow where backlit. Road surface catches specular highlights from the low sun angle.

## Prompt-Ready Description

Late afternoon golden hour with a teal-and-orange cinematic color grade. Warm amber backlight from a low western sun cuts through a partially open forest canopy as diagonal god rays. Haze and mist glow in the backlight. Shadows pushed deep teal-green and slightly lifted, never pure black. Highlights warm with soft rolloff and retained detail. Forest greens desaturated and cooled toward teal-cyan. The Ferrari's red metallic paint stays warm and punchy against the cooler environment. The AMG's matte charcoal reads cool and neutral, receding into the grade. Medium-high filmic contrast, subtle fine film grain, grounded European automotive commercial realism.

## Hard Rules

- Teal-orange split is the backbone. If shadows aren't teal and highlights aren't amber, the grade is off.
- Greens stay desaturated and teal-shifted. Vivid green kills the mood.
- Ferrari red is the warmest thing in frame. Nothing else competes with it in saturation.
- AMG matte grey recedes. This hierarchy is intentional.
