# Scene 01 - Forest Race

## Summary

The forest is calm, then the cars tear through it. A red Ferrari 812 Superfast leads or runs neck-and-neck with a matte charcoal Mercedes-AMG GT3. Rear chase angles dominate because they match the existing references, but the scene needs additional angles now: mirror reveal, roadside pass-by, tunnel compression, detail inserts, and wildlife reaction cuts.

The emotional shape is:

```text
still forest -> Ferrari alone -> AMG threat reveal -> full chase -> AI/weather rupture -> forest reaction -> quiet return
```

## Established Angles (from reference frames)

Most current reference frames are rear chase angles: camera behind and between the cars, at or near road level. This is the dominant visual language, but not the only one.

### Frame 1 — Tunnel Approach

Both cars heading into a stone arch tunnel. Ferrari on the right slightly ahead. AMG on the left, dark grey, partly visible at frame edge. Warm light at the tunnel exit. Road curves gently.

### Frame 2 — Forest Straight, Debris

Both cars on a straight stretch. Heavy atmospheric haze. God rays cutting diagonally from upper right. Leaves and debris kicked up in the air between and behind the cars. Ferrari centered ahead, AMG entering frame left. Most dramatic atmosphere frame.

### Frame 3 — Ferrari Detail, Rear Close-Up

Tight close-up of Ferrari 812 rear quarter. Quad round taillights glowing. Dual exhaust visible. Road rushing beneath. Shallow depth of field — forest blurred. This is the hero car beauty shot.

### Frame 4 — Side-by-Side Chase

Both cars visible from behind, running close together on a gentle curve. AMG on left with rear wing prominent. Ferrari on right. Metal guardrail visible on right side. Soft golden light, misty atmosphere. Cars nearly shoulder-to-shoulder.

## Scene-Specific Overrides

- Road is damp and reflective for this scene. Use tire spray and road sheen.
- Warm foliage is allowed here because existing assets already carry that look.
- Haze, mist, debris, and backlight are load-bearing.
- No visible license plates on either car.
- Sound design is implied through physical reaction: squirrel freeze, mirror vibration, puddle ripples, leaves lifting, branch sway.

## Continuity Notes

- Both cars have NO license plates. Check every generation.
- Ferrari is always the warmer, more visible car in frame.
- AMG is always the darker, more aggressive presence.
- Haze/mist must be present in every shot — it's the connective tissue of this scene.
- Road is slightly damp throughout — no dry asphalt.
- Sun direction is consistent: low western backlight, coming from behind the camera or behind the cars.
- Warm foliage is gold/amber. Avoid vivid grass-green leaves.
