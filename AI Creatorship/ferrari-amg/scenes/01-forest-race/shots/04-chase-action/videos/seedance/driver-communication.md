# Driver Communication Inserts

Use these when the chase needs friendship and human chemistry, not threat. Best workflow: generate the Ferrari-facing insert and the AMG-facing insert as separate clips first, then try the multi-shot version after the faces and cabins are stable.

## v1 - ferrari interior glance ★

Best when you have a Ferrari cabin start frame with the driver already locked.

```text
Continue from the provided start frame. Same Ferrari cabin, same Ferrari driver, same face, same hair, same wardrobe throughout the shot, zero drift. Interior medium close-up from the passenger side of the Ferrari, camera looking across the cabin at the driver's face in the foreground while the car is moving at speed through the forest. The driver keeps both hands engaged, then turns their eyes left toward the car running beside them and breaks into a real spontaneous smile, holding the look for one beat. Through the side window in the background, the matte charcoal AMG paces beside the Ferrari as a moving silhouette with the other driver's face briefly readable through the glass. Road vibration passes through the cabin, seat belt trembles lightly, moving bands of warm forest light and cool shadow sweep across the driver's face, exterior trees streak with speed. No camera drift, no cut, no overtaking, just one clean emotional beat of recognition between drivers. Grounded European automotive commercial realism, late afternoon golden backlight, teal lifted shadows, damp-road reflections flickering through the cabin.
```

## v2 - amg reply

Best as the reverse angle to v1.

```text
Continue from the provided start frame. Same AMG cabin, same AMG driver, same face, same wardrobe throughout the shot, zero drift. Interior medium close-up from the passenger side of the Mercedes-AMG GT3, camera looking across the cockpit at the driver's face in the foreground while the car runs door-to-door with the Ferrari. The driver glances right through the side glass toward the Ferrari beside them and answers with a quick grin, a tiny nod, and focused eyes that return to the road by the end of the shot. In the background outside the window, the Ferrari runs parallel as a warm red metallic shape with the other driver visible only as a soft silhouette inside. Roll cage vibration, harness tension, subtle helmet or hair movement from road energy, reflected tree trunks sliding across glass, pulsing warm backlight and cool cabin shadow. No camera movement, no pass, no aggressive threat energy, only one clean reply beat that reads like friends racing each other for fun.
```

## v3 - side-by-side connector

Harder, but it sells the relationship if Seedance can hold both cabins.

```text
Same Ferrari 812 Superfast and same Mercedes-AMG GT3 throughout the shot, no license plates, no race number. Exterior side-tracking shot from a third vehicle running perfectly parallel with both cars on the damp forest road. The Ferrari and AMG stay door-to-door at matched speed, no overtaking. Through both side windows, the drivers are visible at the same time: the Ferrari driver in the nearer cabin smiling toward the AMG, the AMG driver returning the look for one beat before both lock back onto the road. Keep the faces readable but natural, not exaggerated. Warm backlight cuts through haze behind the cars, reflections travel across side glass, wheels spin with realistic motion blur, tire spray and a few leaves trail low behind. 85mm lens feel, compressed background, real-car-commercial rig energy, no impossible camera move, no drifting camera height.
```

## v4 - three-beat multi-shot

This is the direct answer to the "communication between drivers" beat. Keep it short.

```text
Create multi shot of three beats, all in the same forest race continuity, same Ferrari driver and same AMG driver throughout, zero face drift, same cars throughout, no license plates, no race number. Beat 1 — interior medium close-up inside the Ferrari, passenger-side angle across the cabin, the Ferrari driver glances left and breaks into a quick genuine smile as moving forest light sweeps over the face. Beat 2 — hard cut to interior medium close-up inside the AMG, matching opposite angle, the AMG driver catches the look and answers with a grin and tiny nod while the cockpit vibrates from speed. Beat 3 — hard cut to exterior side-tracking shot of both cars running perfectly side by side, drivers visible through the windows for a brief shared look before eyes return to the road. Same late-afternoon golden backlight, haze, teal shadows, damp asphalt, realistic window reflections, grounded European automotive commercial realism across every beat. Hard cuts only, no dissolves, no overtaking, no crash energy, no extra actions.
```

## v5 - safe silhouette version 📌

Use this when face detail in the background car keeps breaking. It is less ambitious and more reliable.

```text
Continue from the provided start frame. Same Ferrari driver throughout the shot. Tight interior close-up on the Ferrari driver's face in the foreground, three-quarter profile, eyes alive, smiling under concentration while the car moves fast through alternating strips of warm sunlight and cool forest shadow. Outside the side window in the background, the AMG stays alongside only as a dark moving silhouette with the shape of its driver visible through the glass, enough to read that they are racing together and communicating without needing a clean second face. Keep the Ferrari driver's expression subtle and real, one emotional beat only. Cabin vibration, reflected trees sliding across the glass, damp-road flicker, haze glowing outside, no camera move, no pass, no change of car position.
```

**Run Notes**

- Start with `v5` and `v1`. They are the most reliable.
- Run `v2` only after the AMG cabin angle is visually locked.
- Run `v4` last. Multi-shot works better once you know the two single inserts read.
- If the second driver's face keeps collapsing, keep them as a silhouette through glass. The emotion still lands.
