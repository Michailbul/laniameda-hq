## Raw Thought (verbatim)

> Working with AI agents teaches you the same part of the brain that orchestrates work between multiple coworkers — if you had ten of them. The human factor is different, but instead of it there's an AI factor: you have to consider that AI has limitations. It's a different world but very similar.
>
> In the upcoming future, the size of companies where you would have one, two, or three people — basically with only AI agents working for them as employees — will skyrocket. Companies would look like only the founding members. You would not have a lot of people working as employees who don't own a part of the company.
>
> For big companies that may not be the case, but for small ones, for independent creators — a team of six people, the right six people, can get so much done with a team of twenty agents at their disposal.
>
> In order to really be this kind of person, you have to be specifically the artist of how do you create such a system. It's pure art.

---

## Lani's Distillation

**The core insight:**
Managing AI agents activates the same cognitive muscle as managing a human team — but with different failure modes. Instead of the human factor (motivation, personality, politics) you get the AI factor (context limits, hallucination, skill gaps). Different material, same craft.

**The prediction:**
- Future small companies = founding members only + agent workforce
- Equity = for humans. Execution = for agents.
- 6 people + 20 agents > 20 people alone
- The bottleneck shifts from headcount to *how well you orchestrate*

**The meta-skill:**
Building and running an agent team is an art form. The artist is the one who knows how to design the system — who to hire (which agents), what context to give them, how to structure their work, when to intervene. This is not a technical skill. It's a creative and organizational skill.
