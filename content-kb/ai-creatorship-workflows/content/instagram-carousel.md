# Instagram Carousel

> Create high-performing Instagram carousel posts that drive engagement — using the right framing, structure, and CTAs.

## Best workflow right now

This is well-understood from studio research on carousel content strategy.

1. **Define your post archetype first**
   - **Value + Capture** (high engagement): prompts, tutorials, checklists, teardowns, frameworks → CTA: "Comment `KEYWORD` and I'll send the full breakdown"
   - **Brand / Positioning** (trust-building): opinions, founder narrative, news reaction, worldview → CTA: softer (follow, bookmark, reply)

2. **Write a hook that leads with specificity**
   - Generic: "Morning routine tips" → low engagement
   - Specific: "The mistake that kept me stuck for 6 months" → high engagement
   - Pattern: specific mistake > broad advice; lived experience > surface tips

3. **Structure your slides**
   - Slide 1: specific problem or tension (not a title)
   - Slides 2–N: structured breakdown (one idea per slide)
   - Last slide: CTA with keyword
   - Rule: if slide 1 doesn't call out a specific problem, they won't swipe

4. **Set your CTA keyword** (for Value + Capture posts)
   - Suggested keywords by pillar:
     - Prompts: **PROMPT**
     - Agents/automation: **AGENTS**
     - Code/build: **STACK**
     - Marketing frameworks: **PLAYBOOK**
     - Design: **UI**
   - Decide: fixed keywords per pillar (consistent brand recognition) vs. post-specific

5. **Design**
   - Structure drives performance, design supports it
   - Pretty slides with poor structure underperform structured slides with minimal design

## Prompt examples

**Prompt for generating carousel slide titles (AI Creatorship pillar):**
```
Write a 7-slide Instagram carousel about [TOPIC].
Archetype: Value + Capture.
Voice: blunt, first-person, AI creator and builder in public.
Slide 1: specific problem hook (not a title).
Slides 2-6: one concrete idea per slide.
Slide 7: CTA — "Comment PROMPT and I'll send the full breakdown."
No filler phrases. Be specific over generic.
```

## What works
- "Comment KEYWORD" CTA dramatically outperforms passive CTAs for engagement + lead capture
- Specific lived experience framing outperforms broad advice framing (40 likes vs 10k example observed)
- Two-archetype system prevents carousel fatigue — mixing value and brand posts in the feed
- Structure (tension → solve → position) drives performance more than design quality
- "Pretty slides don't grow accounts. Structured ones do." — validated from studied carousel examples

## What doesn't work / gotchas
- Broad topics ("beginner advice") feel safe but underperform
- Vague advice → people won't follow, regardless of design quality
- Using the same CTA style for every post type reduces impact
- Consistency without direction compounds nothing — need clear pillars + hooks
