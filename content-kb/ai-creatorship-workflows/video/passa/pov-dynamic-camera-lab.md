# PASSA — POV Dynamic Camera Lab

> Cold-test Seedance prompts for aggressive POV camera techniques across alpine environments.
> Each prompt = one scene, one camera move, one action. No transitions within a prompt.
> The timeline is the edit plan. The prompts are individual bricks. Cut together in post.

---

## Section 1: SHOT-BY-SHOT EFFECTS TIMELINE (EDIT PLAN)

### SHOT 1 (00:00–00:02) — Floral Curve Entry
- **EFFECT:** Low skim tracking + foreground motion blur smear
- Camera races inches above a dense field of pink and white alpine wildflowers, aggressive forward glide toward a sweeping mountain road curve
- Ultra-low lens height, wide-angle feel, flowers streak heavily across the foreground
- No car. Pure POV speed. The viewer is moving before they know what they're watching.

### SHOT 2 (00:02–00:04) — Car Reveal on the Curve
- **EFFECT:** Low front 3/4 tracking with flower foreground
- Ferrari 812 cornering at speed, flowers blurring in the foreground edges, petals spiraling in the slipstream
- Paint catching full alpine sunlight — bright amber-red
- Clean premium reveal — the car owns the frame

### SHOT 3 (00:04–00:06) — Forest Compression
- **EFFECT:** Light-strobe through canopy + tight framing
- Car races through dense alpine pine forest road, canopy tunnel, golden light shafts strobing across the hood
- Paint shifts to deep burgundy in the shadow
- Claustrophobic framing — speed feels faster because reference points are closer

### SHOT 4 (00:06–00:08) — Mountain Breakout
- **EFFECT:** Wide framing + open sky + scale
- Car on an open alpine mountain road, vast valley, snow peaks, flower meadows
- Paint flares amber-red in full sunlight
- Generous framing — the car is a precision instrument on a massive stage

### SHOT 5 (00:08–00:09) — Aerial Wide
- **EFFECT:** Geographic scale reveal
- Aerial looking down, the car is a red dot on a thin road through the alpine landscape
- Full scale: switchbacks, forests, flower fields, peaks

### SHOT 6 (00:09–00:10) — Dive to Road
- **EFFECT:** FPV plunge from altitude to road level
- Camera dives from high to road surface behind the car
- The $100K shot — helicopter-to-FPV handoff in one take

### SHOT 7 (00:10–00:12) — Hairpin Flyby
- **EFFECT:** Static camera, side-profile pass at wheel height
- Gravel flicks toward lens, suspension compression, doppler flyby
- The viewer flinches — it's physical

### SHOT 8 (00:12–00:14) — Wake Shot
- **EFFECT:** Static hold, environmental aftermath
- Car passes as a blur, petals rip, grass flattens, then everything settles
- The PASSA thesis in one shot: the car passes, the world bends, then heals

## Section 2: PRODUCTION NOTES

**Environment tension pattern:** OPEN → TIGHT → OPEN → WIDE → TIGHT → STILL. Compression and release alternating. Every tight section makes the next open section feel bigger.

**Light system:** Bright alpine sun in open. Cool dappled strobe in forest. Car paint is the barometer — amber-red in sun, burgundy in shade.

**Sound arc:** Wind rush → engine reveal → enclosed reverb → open roar → aerial silence → flyby doppler → fade to quiet.

**Brick rule:** Each prompt does ONE thing. One environment, one camera behavior, one action. No scene transitions within a prompt. Quality over ambition per clip.

1. **Prompt 1** (flower skim, no car) — does the POV speed technique work at all?
2. **Prompt 2** (car + flowers) — does the car hold up with flower foreground?
3. **Prompt 3** (forest) — tight environment compression test
4. **Prompt 7** (hairpin) — static camera, safest ask, tests car physics
5. **Prompt 4** (wide alpine) — open scale test
6. **Prompt 5** (aerial) — does the red dot read?
7. **Prompt 6** (dive) — the hero test, most likely to need iteration
8. **Prompt 8** (wake) — the closer, tests environment reaction physics
