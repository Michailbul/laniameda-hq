# Juice: ImagineArt Seedance 2.0 Prompt Guide

## What to Keep

### 1. Core prompt order is worth locking in
**Subject → Action → Camera → Style**

That sequence is clean and practical. It aligns with how Seedance responds best when the prompt is readable and operational instead of poetic.

### 2. One clear action per shot is a strong rule
The guide reinforces a useful pattern:
- one clear verb
- one readable beat
- one main camera intention

This reduces mushy motion and overstuffed shots.

### 3. Shot vocabulary is useful as a working lexicon
Useful additions for the internal Seedance knowledge base:
- wide
- medium
- close-up
- pan
- dolly / track
- handheld
- locked-off
- gimbal-smooth commercial language

These are especially useful when translating vague creative intent into model-readable shot direction.

### 4. The real value is the categorized library
The 70 prompts are useful less as copy-paste prompts and more as:
- category references
- pattern examples
- retrieval material for future prompting
- raw data for extracting reusable structures

## Where Our Internal Skill Should Stay Smarter

### Continuity lock should be conditional, not universal
Not every Seedance prompt needs `same character throughout all shots`.
For commercial work it may be:
- same car
- same dog
- same product

For montages, forcing continuity can actually hurt.

### Single-shot prompts should stay available
The guide leans toward structured prompting, but in practice some fast iterations work better as compact one-shot prompts without timecodes.

### Physics and environment reaction are underemphasized
Our internal improvements are stronger here:
- suspension compression
- tire grip
- side mirror vibration
- petals / leaves dragged in the slipstream
- debris wake after the vehicle passes
- fabric inertia
- water surface tension

This is where realism and speed perception get much better.

### Commercial montage prompting needs its own mode
The article covers categories, but not enough ad-specific logic.
For pet fashion, automotive inserts, and product coverage, prompts should explicitly support:
- hero silhouette shot
- texture / material detail shot
- playful or emotional contrast shot
- final hero moment / product payoff
